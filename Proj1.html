The Problem

At IntouchCX, operations teams were spending over 15 hours per week manually downloading CSV files from Five9 (our telephony system) and Assembled (our workforce management system). By the time the data was aggregated in Excel to check agent occupancy and Service Level Agreements (SLAs), the information was already outdated. This lag prevented leadership from making rapid, intra-day staffing adjustments during unexpected peak call volumes.

Data Architecture & Workflow

To eliminate the manual downloads, I designed an automated data pipeline. By utilizing APIs, the raw telephony and scheduling data is extracted, transformed to ensure matching primary keys (Agent IDs), and loaded into a centralized structure before being visualized in Looker Studio.

The Solution

I developed a dynamic, interactive dashboard using Looker Studio tailored specifically for non-technical stakeholders (Team Leads and Operations Managers). Key features included:

  • Real-Time Refresh: Connected live data feeds to ensure metrics were never more than 5 minutes old.
  • Unified Metrics: Blended Five9 call data (Handle Time, Wait Time) with Assembled scheduling data (Scheduled vs. Actual Adherence) to create a single source of truth.
  • Threshold Alerts: Implemented conditional formatting to highlight when service levels dropped below 80% or when agent shrinkage exceeded acceptable limits.
